Ghosts of War: Hunt for Pavlichenko is a competitive WWII themed board game where a Soviet Sniper and a team of Nazi Soldiers vie against each other to achieve victory. The game is split up into two teams: the Sniper and the Soldiers. The Sniper team only has one player and their win condition is to eliminate all of the Soldiers. The Soldiers constitute the remaining players (up to 4) and their win condition is to capture the Sniper. The actions are as such:

Sniper’s Actions

  • Move the sniper
  • Shoot a soldier
  • Reload
  • Play an action card

Soldier’s Actions

  • Move a soldier
  • Search a hex
  • Play an action card

Each player on the Soldiers team receives four soldiers that they may control. The Sniper only has their one sniper. At the start of the game, the Soldiers may place their four soldiers in any of the German spawn points. The Sniper also selects their starting spawn position from any of the Soviet spawn points. The Sniper may only shoot a soldier if that soldier is on a diagonal of the Sniper’s current hex and the Sniper has an unobstructed vision to the soldier. Vision may be obstructed by trees and sandbags. Water is impassable terrain but does not obstruct vision. Trees are also impassable terrain. The Sniper occupies a hex on the game board but has no physical representation, thus hiding their position to the Soldiers. The Soldiers are able to determine the Sniper’s position by deducing the vectors on which a shot may have come from. The Soldiers are able to capture the Sniper and win the game if one of their soldiers is occupying the same hex as the Sniper and search the hex with that soldier. The Sniper wins once they have successfully eliminated all of the Soldiers
